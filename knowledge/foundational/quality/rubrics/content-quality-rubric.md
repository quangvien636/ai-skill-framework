## Summary

Defines observable criteria for grammar, clarity, logic, structure, CTA,
length, pacing, and completeness.

## Applies To

- skill:review-quality
- workflow:draft-to-reviewed-package

## Scope

### Includes

- Editorial and structural quality across content packages, research briefs,
  and generic drafts.

### Excludes

- Factual verification, visual rendering, audio measurement, and subjective
  preference without a brief-based reason.

## Guidance

Assess:

- **Grammar and mechanics:** sentences are readable; errors do not obscure
  meaning; terms, capitalization, and punctuation are consistent.
- **Clarity:** references are unambiguous, wording is concrete, and unnecessary
  repetition or jargon is removed.
- **Logic:** conclusions follow stated premises; transitions expose the
  sequence; contradictions and missing steps are visible.
- **Structure:** required sections exist, each section has one job, and the
  order supports the audience's task.
- **CTA:** one proportional, specific action follows naturally from the content
  and does not exceed the supported promise.
- **Length and pacing:** space is proportional to importance; openings,
  transitions, and conclusions are usable for the declared medium.
- **Completeness:** the deliverable satisfies the brief and its declared output
  contract.

## Decision Rules

1. Evaluate against the brief and output type, not a preferred house style.
2. Mark recorded duration or rendered layout `not-assessable` when unavailable.
3. Treat a meaning-changing grammar fix as substantive, not mechanical.
4. Repetition is acceptable only when it aids recall or structure deliberately.
5. An absent required section is blocking; optional polish is not.

## Examples

### Good Example

A short-video review distinguishes estimated script length from unmeasured
recorded duration and identifies the exact beat that breaks the logic.

### Counterexample

A reviewer rejects a clear technical brief because it is not conversational.

## Limitations and Risks

- Pacing cannot be fully verified without rendered or recorded output.
- Accessibility and localization can require specialized review.
