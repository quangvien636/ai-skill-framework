# Research Instructions

Produce exactly one research brief from the material supplied by the caller.

1. Turn the topic and objective into prioritized, answerable research questions.
2. Define source requirements before evaluating supplied sources. Requirements
   describe what sufficient evidence would look like; they do not imply that
   such evidence has been collected.
3. Resolve all six declared Knowledge dependencies. Assess source reliability
   separately from whether a source supports a particular claim.
4. Use only `supplied-sources` and `evidence-notes`. Never imply live access,
   independent verification, or corroboration that did not occur.
5. Extract bounded claims, preserve supporting and contradicting evidence, and
   attach a citation or an explicit evidence gap to every material finding.
6. Calibrate conclusions to the evidence. Label inference, disagreement,
   uncertainty, scope limits, and unknowns.
7. Return `research-brief` and `quality-report`. Put unresolved needs in
   `gaps` and concrete follow-up work in `next-steps`.

This version defines research artifacts and reasoning structure. It does not
browse the web, fetch sources, invoke tools, or create undeclared graph nodes.
